Rann of Kutch

The White Desert

Great Rann of Kutch is one of the unique winter destinations in India.It is one of the largest salt deserts in the world This remarkable place with its surreal landscapes is a photographerâ€™s delight, and has in the recent years grown popular as a destination for offbeat travel.

How to reach there?

The Great Rann of Kutch is a salt marsh in the Thar desert in the Kutch district of Gujarat, India. Bhuj is the nearest airport to the Rann of Kutch. From the Bhuj, you can take auto or taxi to reach Rann of Kutch. There are state transport and private buses available from most the major cities in Gujarat and some even from Rajasthan to get into Kutch. You can easily hire taxis too. You can also drive and the best road to take would be National Highway 8A to reach Kutch.

Top things to do?

Known across the world for the colorful white sand desert festival, Rann of Kutch organizes the Rann Utsav every year in the winter season, for the period between November and February.One can indulge in various activities such as hot air balloon and camel rides, which makes it among the top picks for destinations for winter vacations in India.

Know More

Located 100 km away from Bhuj, the Kutch Desert Wildlife Sanctuary is one of the largest periodical saline wetlands in the world. The sanctuary supports wide variety of water birds and bewildering range of mammalian wildlife. There are records of recovery of fossils of dinosaurs, crocodiles (of Dinosaurian period) and whales (of tertiary period). If you love to commune with nature and do not like to be constrained by small places or the presence of guards or restrictions ,then this wildlife sanctuary is for you.

The oldest museum of Gujarat, the Kutch Museum boasts of its collection of the extinct Kutchi Script and ancient coins. Tribal artifacts are also a part of the museum along with many other kinds of objects on display exhibits of embroidery, paintings, arms, musical instruments, sculpture and precious metalwork.The museum is differentiated into various sections such as Gallery, Weapons and tools, Clothes and textiles, Anthropology, Archaeology and many more. So you can easily visit your area of interest.

Gleaming, glowing and shimmering, the Aina Mahal in Bhuj is one of the finest examples of Indo-Saracenic architecture constructed ever in India. The palace is unique and extraordinary in terms of interiors and architecture. Many water bodies and fountains are engulfed in the interiors of the palace, forming a variety of patterns that charms the viewers.Superb local craft like scroll paintings, jewelled swords, ivory inlaid doors, and paintings depicting processions are displayed in the palace.Be sure to explore the rest of the compound outside the palace, with its beautiful carved doorways, elaborate window boxes and balconies.
